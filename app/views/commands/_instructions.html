Welcome to Rover HQ, Commander.

Our Rovers are ready to explore Gladiator Plateau and we need your navigational skills to ensure success.

We must upload and transmit a command list to the Rover Dispatcher Drone in orbit around Mars

Here are the operational parameters of your mission:

  • The command list must be a text file
  • The command list must be in the following format:
    • The first line of the file specifies the Northeastern point of Gladiator Plateau in the format X Y. Gladiator Plateau is rectangular and we have designated the southwestern point as coordinates 0, 0
    • Each pair of lines thereafter will issue commands to one rover.
    • The first line of the rover command gives the starting coordinates(X Y) and direction(D) in the format X Y D. Valid directions are N, S, E, W
    • The second line of the rover command issues turn and move orders
    • Valid Move Orders:
      • L: Turn left 90 degrees
      • R: Turn right 90 degrees
      • M: Move forward one coordinate in the direction currently facing

  • The following is an example:
    • 5 5 - States that Gladiator Plateau is a 6x6 rectangle (SW point at 0,0 and NE point at 5,5)
    • 1 2 N - Drops the first rover at 1, 2 facing North
    • LMLMLMLMM - Issues commands to rover 1
    • 3 3 E - Drops the second rover at 3, 3 facing East
    • MMRMMRMRRM - Issues commands to rover 2

  • All rovers will be dropped at their starting positions before any commands are transmitted. The rovers will then complete their issued commands in sequence with each rover completing their movement before the next begins
